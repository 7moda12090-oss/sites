Cardiovascular system benefits
Larger stroke volume
The heart's left ventricle expands, pumping more blood per beat — delivering more oxygen to muscles with less effort.
Lower resting heart rate
A trained heart beats fewer times per minute at rest — 50–60 bpm vs the untrained average of 70–80 bpm.
Improved capillary density
Endurance work grows new capillaries in muscle tissue, speeding oxygen delivery and waste removal at the cellular level.
Higher VO₂ max
Your body's max oxygen-use capacity rises — the single best marker of cardiovascular fitness and longevity.
Reduced blood pressure
Regular aerobic calisthenics lowers both systolic and diastolic pressure, reducing long-term risk of heart disease.
Better fat metabolism
The body learns to burn fat as a primary fuel source during sustained effort, preserving glycogen and accelerating fat loss.
