01
Starter
L-Sit
Aka: The Foundation Hold

Both hands press into a surface, arms locked straight, hips raised and legs held parallel to the ground — forming a perfect "L". The gateway static skill that builds core compression, tricep strength, and hip flexor endurance.

Time to Learn 1 – 3 months
Difficulty 1 / 6
Target Hold 10 – 30 sec
Key Muscles Hip Flexors · Triceps · Core
02
Beginner
Elbow Lever
Aka: The Crow's Gateway

The entire body balances horizontally on bent elbows pressing into the hip bones, arms acting as a shelf. Surprisingly accessible once you find the sweet spot — it's mostly a balance trick, but requires real core rigidity and wrist strength.

Time to Learn 1 – 4 months
Difficulty 2 / 6
Target Hold 5 – 15 sec
Key Muscles Core · Shoulders · Wrists
03
Intermediate
Handstand
Aka: The Vertical Throne

Freestanding inverted balance on both hands — perfectly straight body, active shoulders, and constant micro-adjustments through the wrists and fingers. One of the most rewarding skills in all of movement, demanding weeks of wall work before going free.

Time to Learn 3 – 12 months
Difficulty 3 / 6
Target Hold 10 – 60 sec
Key Muscles Shoulders · Core · Wrists · Balance
04
Advanced
90° Hold
Aka: The V-Sit / Parallel Bar Lever

On parallel bars, arms locked straight, body upright — legs extended horizontally at exactly 90° to the torso. The extreme compression demand on hip flexors and lower abs, combined with tricep lockout strength, makes this a true advanced benchmark.

Time to Learn 6 – 18 months
Difficulty 4 / 6
Target Hold 3 – 10 sec
Key Muscles Hip Flexors · Triceps · Lower Abs · Lats
05
Elite
Planche
Aka: The Gravity Defier

The full body held horizontal above the ground, arms straight, with no legs touching any surface. Pure protraction strength, anterior deltoid dominance, and total body tension. Years of progressive training through tuck → advanced tuck → straddle → full planche.

Time to Learn 1 – 4 years
Difficulty 5 / 6
Target Hold 1 – 5 sec (full)
Key Muscles Anterior Delts · Chest · Core · Triceps
06
Legendary
Dragon Planche
Aka: The Apex. The End Game.

The planche's most brutal evolution — hands positioned further forward past the hips, demanding exponentially greater anterior deltoid and wrist extensor strength. The lever arm becomes catastrophically long. Fewer than 0.1% of athletes ever achieve this. A lifetime pursuit.

Time to Learn 4 – 10+ years
Difficulty 6 / 6
Target Hold 1 – 3 sec
Key Muscles Entire Upper Body · Tendons · Will
